# # AI Consumer Safety Framework for Skincare Tools

The tool should clearly explain:

- That AI outputs are not medical conclusions
- That results may vary
- That image quality can affect results
- That professional advice may be needed
- That product suitability depends on individual skin response

## Principle 4: Safer Product Guidance

AI-supported product guidance should be cautious and consumer-friendly.

Responsible guidance should:

- Avoid aggressive product recommendations
- Warn against overuse of strong actives
- Encourage patch testing where appropriate
- Explain potential irritation risks
- Avoid unsafe ingredient combinations
- Promote barrier-supportive skincare habits

## Principle 5: Human Oversight

AI should support human decision-making, not replace it.

Human oversight is important for:

- Reviewing tool logic
- Monitoring consumer feedback
- Updating guidance when needed
- Ensuring responsible language
- Preventing misleading claims

## Principle 6: Consumer Education

A strong skincare AI tool should educate users, not confuse them.

Education may include:

- General skincare explanations
- Ingredient awareness
- Routine-building guidance
- Product category explanations
- When to seek professional support
- How to track skin changes over time

## Principle 7: Ethical Communication

Beauty-tech tools should communicate in a way that builds trust.

This means avoiding:

- Fear-based marketing
- Overpromising results
- Medical claims without evidence
- One-size-fits-all recommendations
- Language that makes consumers feel excluded or ashamed

## Proposed Use Case

A responsible non-clinical skincare decision-support tool could help a user:

- Understand general cosmetic concerns
- Learn about common skincare ingredients
- Track routine consistency
- Identify possible product mismatch
- Avoid overuse of irritating products
- Recognise when professional advice may be needed

## Conclusion

AI has the potential to improve skincare education and consumer confidence, but only when designed responsibly. For melanin-rich consumers, inclusive data, transparent limitations, and consumer safety must be central to the design process.
